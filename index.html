Rules

Evaluation Rules

1 Profit Target

You must reach the profit target percentage within the time limit. One-Step requires 10%, Two-Step requires 6% (Phase 1) then 4% (Phase 2).

2 Maximum Drawdown

Your account cannot drop more than 6% from its high-water mark at any point. If it does, the evaluation is immediately failed.

3 Consistency Rule

No single winning trade can account for more than the allowed percentage of your total profits. This prevents lucky one-shot traders.

One-Step consistency cap20% of total profit
Two-Step consistency cap50% of total profit

4 Minimum Trades

You must complete the minimum number of trades before the evaluation can pass. One-Step requires 5 trades, Two-Step requires 2 per phase.

5 Position Sizing

No single position can exceed 15% of your current account balance. This ensures proper risk management.

6 Time Limit

Each phase has a 30-day time limit. You must hit all targets within this window or the evaluation expires.

What Are Prediction-Based Markets?

01

What Are Prediction Markets?

Prediction markets are exchange-traded markets where participants buy and sell contracts that pay out based on the outcome of future events. Each contract resolves to $1.00 if the event occurs and $0.00 if it doesn't. The current trading price represents the market's collective estimate of the probability of that event happening.

For example, if a contract "Will X happen by December?" trades at $0.65, the market collectively estimates a 65% probability of that event occurring.

02

How They Work

Participants trade binary outcome contracts on a prediction market exchange. You can buy "Yes" shares if you believe the event will happen, or "No" shares if you believe it won't. Prices fluctuate between $0.01 and $0.99 based on supply and demand, reflecting the market's evolving probability estimate.

When the event resolves, winning shares pay out $1.00 each, and losing shares expire worthless. Your profit is the difference between your entry price and the $1.00 payout, minus any trading fees.

03

Risk Profile

Prediction markets carry defined risk. When you buy a contract at $0.65, your maximum loss is the $0.65 you paid, and your maximum profit is $0.35 (the $1.00 payout minus your cost). This binary structure means you always know your worst-case scenario before entering a position.

However, prices can move against you before resolution, creating unrealized losses. Position sizing and portfolio diversification are critical risk management tools.

04

Expected Value

Profitable prediction market trading is fundamentally about finding mispriced contracts, situations where the true probability of an event differs from the price the market assigns. If you believe an event has a 75% chance of occurring but the market prices it at 60%, buying that contract has positive expected value.

Expected value (EV) = (Probability × Payout) − Cost. Consistent profitability requires disciplined analysis and a systematic approach to identifying edge.

05

Market Inefficiencies

Prediction markets, like all markets, can be inefficient. Inefficiencies arise from information asymmetry, behavioral biases (overconfidence, anchoring, recency bias), liquidity imbalances, and delayed reactions to breaking information. These inefficiencies create opportunities for informed traders.

Common patterns include slow price adjustments after news events, overreaction to unlikely tail risks, and systematic mispricing in niche or low-volume markets.

06

How Traders Profit

Successful prediction market traders combine deep domain expertise with disciplined risk management. They analyze events using primary sources, statistical models, and domain-specific knowledge to develop independent probability estimates. When those estimates diverge meaningfully from market prices, they trade.

Key principles include diversifying across uncorrelated events, sizing positions according to confidence level, and continuously updating probability estimates as new information emerges.

07

Potential Upside

Prediction markets are a rapidly growing asset class with expanding coverage across politics, economics, sports, technology, and current events. As these markets mature, liquidity deepens and the range of tradable events expands.

For skilled analysts with domain expertise, prediction markets offer a unique opportunity to monetize knowledge and analytical ability. Unlike traditional financial markets, prediction markets reward subject-matter expertise directly. If you understand politics, science, technology, or any other domain better than the average market participant, you have a structural edge.
